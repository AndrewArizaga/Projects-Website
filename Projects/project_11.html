Low-Power Two-Step Successive Approximation ADC

This project develops a low-power 10-bit SAR ADC using a hybrid architecture consisting of a 6-bit voltage-mode SAR ADC, a Voltage-to-Time Converter (VTC), and a 4-bit Vernier Time-to-Digital Converter (TDC). Designed for applications in wireless sensors and biomedical instruments, it emphasizes high efficiency and low power consumption.

Project Description

Overview

This project addresses the challenge of developing a low-power 10-bit SAR ADC for applications in biomedical sensors and wireless systems. The design integrates a 6-bit SAR ADC, a Voltage-to-Time Converter (VTC), and a 4-bit Vernier Time-to-Digital Converter (TDC), achieving high resolution and low power consumption.

Key Features

  • Hybrid SAR ADC architecture for enhanced performance.
  • Low-power design, ideal for battery-operated devices.
  • High resolution with an Effective Number of Bits (ENOB) of 10.
  • Built using TSMC 130 nm CMOS technology.

How It Works

Step 1: Input Voltage Sampling

The system begins with the 6-bit SAR ADC performing coarse voltage sampling. The residual voltage is then mapped to a time delay using the Voltage-to-Time Converter (VTC).

Step 2: Time-to-Digital Conversion

The VTC output is digitized using a 4-bit Vernier Time-to-Digital Converter (TDC), ensuring high precision. The combined outputs of the SAR ADC and TDC form the 10-bit digital signal.

Applications and Benefits

Biomedical Sensors

Enables accurate measurement of physiological signals such as ECG and EEG with minimal power consumption.

Wireless Sensor Networks

Optimized for battery-operated wireless nodes, enhancing network longevity and performance.

Technical Challenges

Design Complexity

Balancing high resolution with low power consumption required innovative circuit designs and extensive simulations.

Post-Layout Validation

Ensuring the performance of the circuit after layout implementation posed significant challenges due to parasitics.
