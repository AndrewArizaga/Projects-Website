Coursework

Fall 2023

CEN 100Introduction to Engineering

This course introduces first-year students to the academic structure and expectations of engineering. Topics include public and worker safety, health impacts of engineering activities, safety standards, and ethical responsibilities of engineers. Case studies illustrate various engineering fields.

CHY 102General Chemistry

Designed for engineering students, this course covers stoichiometry, states of matter, chemical equilibria, thermodynamics, kinetics, nuclear chemistry, and electrochemistry. Emphasis is placed on problem-solving and calculations.

MTH 140Calculus I

Covers limits, continuity, differentiability, rules of differentiation, extrema, inflection points, curve sketching, integration, and applications of transcendental functions.

MTH 141Linear Algebra

Explores Euclidean spaces, matrices, determinants, linear transformations, eigenvalues, complex numbers, and vector spaces, with applications in engineering.

PCS 211Physics: Mechanics

Topics include forces, equilibrium conditions, Newton's laws, kinematics, work, energy, and momentum. The course emphasizes foundational mechanics in engineering contexts.

GEO 793The Geography of Toronto

Examines Toronto’s development, from its founding on First Nations territories to its evolution as Canada’s largest metropolitan area. Explores historical, social, cultural, political, and environmental aspects of the city.

CMN 432Communication in the Engineering Professions

Focuses on communication challenges in engineering. Students refine analytical, writing, presentation, and problem-solving skills through lectures, workshops, and simulations.

SPN 201Introductory Spanish II

Enhances oral and written Spanish skills while introducing cultural and historical aspects of Spanish-speaking regions through readings.
