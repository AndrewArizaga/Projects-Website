Coursework

Fall 2023

CEN 100: Introduction to Engineering

This foundational course introduces first-year engineering students to key concepts, including engineering principles, safety standards, ethics, and the integration of theory with practical applications. It lays the groundwork for future studies by fostering problem-solving skills and an understanding of the multidisciplinary nature of engineering.

CHY 102: General Chemistry

Explores chemical reactions and processes with a focus on stoichiometry, thermodynamics, kinetics, and equilibrium. Emphasis is placed on real-world engineering applications such as corrosion prevention, material selection, and environmental considerations.

MTH 140: Calculus I

Provides a thorough understanding of fundamental calculus concepts, including limits, derivatives, integrals, and their applications. The course incorporates engineering-specific problems to illustrate the utility of calculus in design and analysis.

MTH 141: Linear Algebra

Introduces vector spaces, matrix operations, eigenvalues, and eigenvectors. Applications include solving engineering systems, optimization, and modeling real-world phenomena using linear transformations.

PCS 211: Physics: Mechanics

Covers fundamental principles of mechanics, including Newton's laws, work, energy, and rotational motion. The course emphasizes practical applications in structural engineering, dynamics, and materials science.

CMN 432: Communication in the Engineering Professions

Develops professional communication skills through technical writing, oral presentations, and collaborative projects. Focuses on effectively conveying complex engineering ideas to diverse audiences.

GEO 793: The Geography of Toronto

Explores Toronto's geographic evolution, including urban planning, socioeconomic changes, and environmental challenges. The course also addresses sustainable development in the context of urban growth.

SPN 201: Introductory Spanish II

Enhances language skills in reading, writing, speaking, and listening. The course integrates cultural studies of Spanish-speaking countries, fostering a deeper understanding of their traditions and histories.

Winter 2024

PCS 125: Physics: Waves and Fields

Explores the principles of wave mechanics, including harmonic motion, wave interference, and the Doppler effect. Topics also include gravitational, electric, and magnetic fields with practical engineering applications.

MTH 240: Calculus II

Expands on the foundations of Calculus I, introducing advanced integration techniques, partial derivatives, and differential equations. Real-world examples illustrate its application in engineering systems and fluid dynamics.

ELE 202: Electric Circuit Analysis

Introduces fundamental electrical circuit concepts, including transient and steady-state analysis, AC power, and resonance. The course emphasizes hands-on problem-solving and simulation tools.

CPS 188: Computer Programming Fundamentals

Teaches programming principles using C language, focusing on algorithms, data structures, and software design. Projects emphasize practical problem-solving in an engineering context.

CEN 800: Law and Ethics in Engineering Practice

Examines the legal and ethical responsibilities of engineers, including contract law, intellectual property, and environmental regulations. Case studies provide practical insights into real-world engineering challenges.

CPS 109: Computer Science I

Provides a solid foundation in computer science, covering abstraction, algorithms, and object-oriented programming. The course emphasizes developing efficient and reliable code.

GEO 702: Technology and the Contemporary Environment

Analyzes the intersection of technology and environmental issues, focusing on renewable energy, urban planning, and sustainable development.

ENT 401: Design Thinking Experience

Introduces design thinking methodologies through hands-on projects. Students develop innovative solutions to real-world problems, from ideation to prototyping.

Spring/Summer 2024

MTH 312: Differential Equations and Vector Calculus

Focuses on solving differential equations and applying vector calculus to physical systems. Applications include electric circuits, fluid flow, and heat transfer.

MTH 314: Discrete Mathematics for Engineers

Explores combinatorics, graph theory, Boolean algebra, and finite-state machines. The course emphasizes their application in algorithm design and network analysis.

Fall 2024

MTH 510: Numerical Analysis

COE 328: Digital Systems

CPS 209: Computer Science II

MTH 40A: Special Topics in Mathematics
